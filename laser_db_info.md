4.2 Genetic Algorithms for Optimization

Genetic algorithms (GAs) are widely used optimization algorithms inspired by the principles of natural selection and evolution. The GA process involves several key steps:

4.2.1 Initialization: A population of individuals is randomly generated, with each individual representing a set of laser parameters. The population size is determined based on the problem complexity and computational resources available.

4.2.2 Evaluation: Each individual in the population is evaluated based on a fitness function that quantifies the quality of the color marking achieved with the corresponding laser parameters. The fitness function considers color accuracy, saturation, brightness, and other relevant factors.

4.2.3 Selection: Individuals with higher fitness values are more likely to be selected for reproduction. Selection methods such as roulette wheel selection, tournament selection, or rank-based selection are applied to determine the parents for the next generation.

4.2.4 Reproduction: Reproduction involves creating offspring from selected individuals through crossover and mutation operations. 4.2.5 Replacement: The offspring replace a portion of the existing population, ensuring that the population size remains constant. Replacement strategies such as elitism or generational replacement determine which individuals are replaced by the offspring.

The PSO process involves the following steps:

4.3.1 Initialization: A population of particles is randomly initialized, with each particle representing a set of laser parameters. The population size is determined based on the problem complexity and computational resources available.

4.3.3 Updating Particle Velocity and Position: Each particle adjusts its velocity based on its own experience and the influence of the best-performing particle in its neighborhood. 4.3.5 Termination Criteria: The PSO process continues until a termination condition is met, such as reaching a maximum number of iterations or achieving a satisfactory fitness level. The particle with the best fitness value represents the optimal laser parameter set for color marking.
